# Topics Research

## Question
What are the strongest arguments against preterism, futurism, and idealism as interpretive frameworks for Revelation? Why does each fail to account for the evidence?

## Topics

## Related Topics Discovered

### Key Observations for This Study
1. **BABYLON** topic includes both literal/historical and figurative uses -- REV 14:8; 16:19; 17; 18 are listed as "FIGURATIVE" uses, distinct from the literal city/empire
2. **CHURCH** topic applies Israel identity titles (Israel of God, Holy Nation, Kingdom of God) to the church, directly relevant to futurism's Israel/Church distinction
3. **PROPHECY** topic emphasizes sure fulfillment (EZK 12:22-25,28; HAB 2:3) and proof of God's foreknowledge (ISA 43:9), relevant to idealism's claim that prophecy lacks specific referents
4. **TIME** topic specifically includes Daniel's "time, times, and half a time" (DAN 7:25; 12:7) and the day-as-thousand-years principle (2PE 3:8), directly relevant to prophetic time periods
5. **JUDGMENT** topic includes the progression from DAN 7:9,10 through REV 20:11-15, showing a sequence from investigation to final execution
