### Historical Context from Web Research

**538 AD — Start of the 1260-Year Period:**
- Siege of Rome (537-538): Belisarius, commanding Justinian's Byzantine forces, defended Rome against the Ostrogoth army under King Vitiges. On March 12, 538, Vitiges abandoned the siege after 374 days.
- Pope Vigilius ascended the papal chair under Belisarius's military protection in 538, after Pope Silverius was deposed.
- Justinian had addressed the pope as "head of all the churches" in his decree of 533 AD, but actual implementation of papal temporal supremacy began in 538 when the last Arian opposition (Ostrogoths) was broken.
- Sources: [Siege of Rome (537-538) — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Rome_(537%E2%80%93538)); [538 A.D. and the Transition — IJHSS](https://ijhss.thebrpi.org/journals/Vol_7_No_1_January_2017/7.pdf)

**1798 AD — End of the 1260-Year Period:**
- On February 10, 1798, French General Louis-Alexandre Berthier marched into Rome, proclaimed a Roman Republic, and demanded that Pope Pius VI renounce his temporal authority.
- On February 20, 1798, Pope Pius VI was formally arrested at the Quirinal Palace and placed under military custody.
- Pius VI refused to renounce his temporal authority and was taken prisoner, transported through Italy and France, and died in captivity at Valence on August 29, 1799.
- This marked the end of papal temporal power until its partial restoration (the "deadly wound healed" of Rev 13:3).
- Sources: [When Napoleon's forces kidnapped the Pope — History Skills](https://www.historyskills.com/classroom/modern-history/berthier-and-the-pope/); [Pope Pius VI — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_Pius_VI); [1798: Pope Captured — History.info](https://history.info/on-this-day/1798-pope-captured-rome/)
