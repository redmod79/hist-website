# Verses

## Question
How do NT authors treat Daniel 7-12? Do Jesus, Paul, and John/Revelation treat these chapters as independent visions or as one unified prophetic system?

- v.1: "things which must shortly come to pass" = Dan 2:28 LXX "ha dei genesthai"
- v.7: "cometh with clouds" = Dan 7:13 "one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven"

- v.14: "white like wool, as white as snow" = Dan 7:9 "whose garment was white as snow, and the hair of his head like the pure wool"
- v.15: "voice as the sound of many waters" = Dan 10:6

**Critical link: Dan 2:28 LXX reads "ha dei genesthai ep' eschatou ton hemeron" ("what must come to pass in the last days"). Rev 1:1 reads "ha dei genesthai en tachei" ("what must come to pass shortly"). The core formula "ha dei genesthai" is RETAINED; "in the last days" is REPLACED with "shortly."**

### Revelation 22:6-10 (Closing Frame / Inclusio)
> 22:6 And he said unto me, These sayings are faithful and true: and the Lord God of the holy prophets sent his angel to shew unto his servants the things which must shortly be done.
> 22:7 Behold, I come quickly: blessed is he that keepeth the sayings of the prophecy of this book.
> 22:8 And I John saw these things, and heard them. And when I had heard and seen, I fell down to worship before the feet of the angel which shewed me these things.
> 22:9 Then saith he unto me, See thou do it not: for I am thy fellowservant, and of thy brethren the prophets, and of them which keep the sayings of this book: worship God.
> **22:10 And he saith unto me, Seal not the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for the time is at hand.**

**Inclusio structure:**
- Rev 1:1 and Rev 22:6: identical phrase "ha dei genesthai en tachei" frames the entire book
- Rev 1:3 and Rev 22:10: matching "ho kairos engys" ("the time is near") forms a second frame
- Rev 22:10 "Seal NOT" directly reverses Dan 12:4 "seal the book"

## 2. The Sealed-to-Unsealed Arc

### Daniel 8:26
> And the vision of the evening and the morning which was told is true: wherefore **shut thou up the vision**; for it shall be for many days.

### Daniel 12:4
> But thou, O Daniel, **shut up the words, and seal the book**, even to the time of the end: many shall run to and fro, and knowledge shall be increased.

### Daniel 12:9
> And he said, Go thy way, Daniel: for **the words are closed up and sealed** till the time of the end.

### Revelation 5:1-9 (Unsealing begins)
> 5:1 And I saw in the right hand of him that sat on the throne **a book written within and on the backside, sealed with seven seals**.
> 5:2 And I saw a strong angel proclaiming with a loud voice, **Who is worthy to open the book, and to loose the seals thereof?**
> 5:3 And no man in heaven, nor in earth, neither under the earth, was able to open the book, neither to look thereon.
> 5:4 And I wept much, because no man was found worthy to open and to read the book, neither to look thereon.
> 5:5 And one of the elders saith unto me, Weep not: behold, **the Lion of the tribe of Juda, the Root of David, hath prevailed to open the book**, and to loose the seven seals thereof.
> 5:6 And I beheld, and, lo, in the midst of the throne and of the four beasts, and in the midst of the elders, stood a Lamb as it had been slain, having seven horns and seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth.
> 5:7 And he came and **took the book** out of the right hand of him that sat upon the throne.
> 5:9 And they sung a new song, saying, **Thou art worthy to take the book, and to open the seals thereof**: for thou wast slain, and hast redeemed us to God by thy blood out of every kindred, and tongue, and people, and nation;

### Revelation 22:10 (Unsealing command)
> And he saith unto me, **Seal not** the sayings of the prophecy of this book: for **the time is at hand**.

**The arc: Daniel SEALED (8:26; 12:4,9) --> Revelation UNSEALED (5:1-9; 22:10)**
- Daniel told to seal because "it shall be for many days" (8:26) / "the time of the end" is distant (12:9)
- John told NOT to seal because "the time is at hand" (22:10)
- The Lamb's worthiness to unseal (5:5-9) = the fulfillment era has begun

> 24:5 For many shall come in my name, saying, I am Christ; and shall deceive many.
> 24:6 And ye shall hear of wars and rumours of wars: see that ye be not troubled: for all these things **must** come to pass, but the end is not yet.
> ...
> **24:15 When ye therefore shall see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, stand in the holy place, (whoso readeth, let him understand:)**
> 24:16 Then let them which be in Judaea flee into the mountains:
> ...
> **24:21 For then shall be great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world to this time, no, nor ever shall be.**
> ...
> **24:27 For as the lightning cometh out of the east, and shineth even unto the west; so shall also the coming of the Son of man be.**
> ...
> **24:29 Immediately after the tribulation of those days shall the sun be darkened, and the moon shall not give her light, and the stars shall fall from heaven, and the powers of the heavens shall be shaken:**
> **24:30 And then shall appear the sign of the Son of man in heaven: and then shall all the tribes of the earth mourn, and they shall see the Son of man coming in the clouds of heaven with power and great glory.**

> ...
> 24:35 Heaven and earth shall pass away, but my words shall not pass away.

**Jesus draws from at least four Daniel chapters in one discourse:**
1. **Dan 8-9-11-12**: "the abomination of desolation" (v.15) -- this phrase spans Dan 9:27, 11:31, 12:11, with background in Dan 8:13
2. **Dan 12:1**: "great tribulation, such as was not since the beginning of the world" (v.21) -- nearly verbatim from Dan 12:1 "a time of trouble, such as never was since there was a nation"
3. **Dan 7:13**: "the Son of man coming in the clouds of heaven with power and great glory" (v.30) -- from Dan 7:13 "one like the Son of man came with the clouds of heaven"
4. **Dan 7:14**: "power and great glory" (v.30) echoes Dan 7:14 "there was given him dominion, and glory, and a kingdom"

### Mark 13:14-27
> **13:14 But when ye shall see the abomination of desolation, spoken of by Daniel the prophet, standing where it ought not, (let him that readeth understand,) then let them that be in Judaea flee to the mountains:**
> ...
> **13:19 For in those days shall be affliction, such as was not from the beginning of the creation which God created unto this time, neither shall be.**
> ...
> **13:26 And then shall they see the Son of man coming in the clouds with great power and glory.**
> 13:27 And then shall he send his angels, and shall gather together his elect from the four winds, from the uttermost part of the earth to the uttermost part of heaven.

**Mark 13:14: ἑστηκότα (MASCULINE participle) modifying βδέλυγμα (NEUTER noun) = constructio ad sensum, indicating the "abomination" is a PERSON**

### Luke 21:20-28
> **21:20 And when ye shall see Jerusalem compassed with armies, then know that the desolation thereof is nigh.**
> 21:21 Then let them which are in Judaea flee to the mountains; and let them which are in the midst of it depart out; and let not them that are in the countries enter thereinto.
> 21:22 For these be the days of vengeance, that all things which are written may be fulfilled.
> ...
> **21:27 And then shall they see the Son of man coming in a cloud with power and great glory.**

**Luke replaces the Semitic idiom "abomination of desolation" with "Jerusalem compassed with armies" but RETAINS eremosis (G2050) in v.20 -- the "Daniel marker" word.**

### Matthew 26:64
> Jesus saith unto him, Thou hast said: nevertheless I say unto you, Hereafter shall ye see **the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven**.

### Mark 14:62
> And Jesus said, I am: and ye shall see **the Son of man sitting on the right hand of power, and coming in the clouds of heaven**.

**At his trial, Jesus combines Dan 7:13 (Son of man in clouds) with Psa 110:1 (sitting at God's right hand), claiming both Daniel's heavenly figure and David's Lord as his own identity.**

**John combines ALL FOUR of Daniel 7's beasts into one creature:**

- v.5: **42 months** = Dan 7:25 "time, times, half a time" (3.5 years)
- v.6: blasphemy against God = Dan 7:25 "speak great words against the most High"
- v.7: "war with the saints" = Dan 7:21 "the same horn made war with the saints"

## 6. The Time-Period Chain

### Daniel 7:25 (Aramaic: iddan)
> ...a time and times and the dividing of time.

### Daniel 12:7 (Hebrew: moed)
> ...it shall be for a time, times, and an half; and when he shall have accomplished to scatter the power of the holy people, all these things shall be finished.

### Revelation 12:6 (1,260 days)
> And the woman fled into the wilderness, where she hath a place prepared of God, that they should feed her there a thousand two hundred and threescore days.

### Revelation 12:14 (Greek: kairos)
> And to the woman were given two wings of a great eagle, that she might fly into the wilderness, into her place, where she is nourished for **a time, and times, and half a time**, from the face of the serpent.

### Revelation 11:2-3 (42 months / 1,260 days)
> 11:2 ...the holy city shall they tread under foot **forty and two months**.
> 11:3 And I will give power unto my two witnesses, and they shall prophesy **a thousand two hundred and threescore days**, clothed in sackcloth.

### Revelation 13:5 (42 months)
> ...and power was given unto him to continue **forty and two months**.

**The equivalency chain:**
- Dan 7:25: "time, times, half a time" (Aramaic iddan)
- Dan 12:7: "time, times, and an half" (Hebrew moed)
- Rev 12:14: "time, times, and half a time" (Greek kairos)
- Rev 12:6 = 1,260 days
- Rev 11:2 = 42 months
- Rev 11:3 = 1,260 days
- Rev 13:5 = 42 months
- **All = 3.5 prophetic years** (using 30-day months: 42 x 30 = 1,260)

### Daniel 12:11 (Abomination set up)
> And from the time that the daily sacrifice shall be taken away, and the abomination that maketh desolate set up, there shall be a thousand two hundred and ninety days.

### Revelation 12:1-17 (Full Chapter Context for Time Period)
> 12:6 And the woman fled into the wilderness, where she hath a place prepared of God, that they should feed her there **a thousand two hundred and threescore days**.
> 12:14 ...where she is nourished for **a time, and times, and half a time**, from the face of the serpent.

**Rev 12:6 and 12:14 state the SAME period in two different formats: 1,260 days = time, times, half a time. This confirms the equivalency and links directly to Dan 7:25 and 12:7.**
