# Raw Data: Concept Context

## REV 1:1 — Concept Context (Book Scope)

Concepts in verse: MESSIAH (G5547 Christ)

### Same Chapter (Revelation 1):
- Rev 1:2 — "testimony of Jesus Christ"
- Rev 1:5 — "Jesus Christ, the faithful witness"
- Rev 1:9 — "kingdom and patience of Jesus Christ" — NOTE: hypomonE co-occurs with Christos in the opening chapter

### Same Book (Revelation):
- Rev 11:15 — "kingdoms of this world are become the kingdoms of our Lord, and of his Christ" — prophetic aorist
- Rev 12:10 — "Now is come... the kingdom of our God, and the power of his Christ" — arti egeneto + Christ
- Rev 12:17 — "remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ"
- Rev 20:4 — "they lived and reigned with Christ a thousand years"
- Rev 20:6 — "priests of God and of Christ, and shall reign with him a thousand years"
- Rev 22:21 — "The grace of our Lord Jesus Christ be with you all"

### Significance
The MESSIAH concept threads from Rev 1:1 (the Revelation IS of Jesus Christ) through the entire book to Rev 22:21 (the grace of Jesus Christ). The "en tachei" framework in 1:1 covers all events related to Christ's unfolding kingdom — from present realities (1:9 patience, 12:10 "now is come") to future consummation (20:4-6 millennial reign, 22:20 "I come quickly").

## Key Observation
The concept context tool confirms that Rev 1:9 (hypomonE + Christ) is in the same chapter as Rev 1:1 (en tachei + Christ), creating an immediate literary juxtaposition of "shortly" with "patience" — the very tension this study examines.
