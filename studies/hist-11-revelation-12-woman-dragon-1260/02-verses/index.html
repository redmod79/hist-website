    Revelation 12:12 Therefore rejoice, [ye] heavens, and ye that dwell in them. Woe to the inhabiters of the earth and of the sea! for the devil is come down unto you, having great wrath, because he knoweth that he hath but a short time.

    Revelation 12:13 And when the dragon saw that he was cast unto the earth, he persecuted the woman which brought forth the man [child].

    Revelation 12:15 And the serpent cast out of his mouth water as a flood after the woman, that he might cause her to be carried away of the flood.

    Revelation 12:16 And the earth helped the woman, and the earth opened her mouth, and swallowed up the flood which the dragon cast out of his mouth.

    Revelation 12:17 And the dragon was wroth with the woman, and went to make war with the remnant of her seed, which keep the commandments of God, and have the testimony of Jesus Christ.

    Historical Evidence (from Web Research)¶

    538 AD — Start of the 1260-Year Period¶

    • Siege of Rome (537-538): Belisarius defended Rome against Ostrogoth army under King Vitiges
    • March 12, 538: Vitiges abandoned the siege after 374 days
    • In 533 AD Justinian addressed the pope as "head of all the churches"
    • In 537 Belisarius sent Pope Silverius into exile and installed Vigilius
    • In 538 Justinian's code of papal headship became meaningfully effective
    • Sources: Siege of Rome (537-538); 538 A.D. and the Transition

    1798 AD — End of the 1260-Year Period¶

    • Feb 10, 1798: General Berthier marched into Rome, proclaimed Roman Republic
    • Demanded pope renounce temporal authority
    • Feb 20, 1798: Pope Pius VI formally arrested at Quirinal Palace
    • Pius VI refused to renounce temporal authority, was taken prisoner
    • Died in captivity at Valence, France, Aug 29, 1799
    • Effectively abolished temporal power held since 8th century
    • Sources: When Napoleon's forces kidnapped the Pope; Pope Pius VI

    Waldensian Persecution — The Wilderness Church¶

    • Founded by Peter Waldo in Lyon, France, ~1173
    • 1184: Excommunicated at Synod of Verona
    • 1211: 80+ burned at Strasbourg
    • Fled to remote Alpine valleys (Cottian Alps) — literal wilderness
    • 1487: Pope Innocent VIII pronounced crusade against them
    • 1545: Towns of Merindol and Cabrieres destroyed, ~4,000 killed
    • Sources: Waldensians; Waldenses — Britannica
