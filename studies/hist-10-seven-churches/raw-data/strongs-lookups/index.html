    Strong's Lookups Raw Data¶

    G3528 — νικάω (nikao) — overcome, conquer¶

    Transliteration: nikao | Pronunciation: nik-ah | POS: verb | BLB Count: 28 Definition: to subdue (literally or figuratively) — conquer, overcome KJV Translations (22 occurrences): - "overcometh" (5x): Rev 2:11, 2:17, 2:26, 3:5, 21:7 - "that overcometh" (3x) - "overcome" (2x): Rom 12:21 (x2) - "overcame" (2x): Rev 3:21, 12:11 - "shall overcome" (2x) - "conquering" (1x): Rev 6:2 - "conquer" (1x): Rev 6:2 - "hath prevailed" (1x): Rev 5:5 - "Be" (1x): [John 16:33 "be of good cheer"] - "had gotten the victory" (1x): Rev 15:2 - "to overcome" (1x) - "mightest overcome" (1x)

    Complete Revelation Usage: | Ref | Translation | Context | |-----|------------|---------| | Rev 2:7 | overcometh | Ephesus overcomer promise (tree of life) | | Rev 2:11 | overcometh | Smyrna overcomer promise (second death) | | Rev 2:17 | overcometh | Pergamos overcomer promise (hidden manna) | | Rev 2:26 | overcometh | Thyatira overcomer promise (power over nations) | | Rev 3:5 | overcometh | Sardis overcomer promise (white raiment) | | Rev 3:12 | overcometh | Philadelphia overcomer promise (pillar in temple) | | Rev 3:21 | overcometh / overcame | Laodicea overcomer promise (throne) + "as I also overcame" | | Rev 5:5 | hath prevailed | Lion of Judah prevailed to open seals | | Rev 6:2 | conquering / conquer | First seal rider | | Rev 11:7 | overcome | Beast overcomes two witnesses | | Rev 12:11 | overcame | Saints overcame by blood of Lamb | | Rev 13:7 | overcome | Beast overcomes saints | | Rev 15:2 | gotten the victory | Those who got victory over the beast | | Rev 17:14 | shall overcome | Lamb shall overcome them | | Rev 21:7 | overcometh | Final overcomer capstone |

    G5245 — ὑπερνικάω (hupernikao) — more than conquer¶

    Transliteration: hupernikao | POS: verb | BLB Count: 1 Definition: to vanquish beyond, gain a decisive victory — more than conquer Single occurrence: Rom 8:37 — "we are more than conquerors through him that loved us"

    G3534 — νῖκος (nikos) — victory¶

    Transliteration: nikos | POS: neuter noun | BLB Count: 4 Definition: a conquest (concretely), by implication Occurrences: Mat 12:20; 1 Cor 15:54, 55, 57 — all translated "victory"

    G1577 — ἐκκλησία (ekklesia) — church, assembly¶

    Transliteration: ekklesia | Pronunciation: ek-klay-see | POS: feminine noun | BLB Count: 118 Definition: a calling out; a popular meeting, especially a religious congregation KJV Translations (112 occurrences): - "church" (53x) | "churches" (35x) | "the church" (11x) | "of the church" (8x) | "assembly" (3x)

    Revelation Usage (20 occurrences): | Ref | Translation | Context | |-----|------------|---------| | Rev 1:4 | churches | "seven churches which are in Asia" | | Rev 1:11 | churches | "send it unto the seven churches" | | Rev 1:20 | churches (x2) | "angels of the seven churches" / "seven candlesticks are the seven churches" | | Rev 2:1 | church | "angel of the church of Ephesus" | | Rev 2:7 | churches | "what the Spirit saith unto the churches" | | Rev 2:8 | church | "angel of the church in Smyrna" | | Rev 2:11 | churches | "what the Spirit saith unto the churches" | | Rev 2:12 | church | "angel of the church in Pergamos" | | Rev 2:17 | churches | "what the Spirit saith unto the churches" | | Rev 2:18 | church | "angel of the church in Thyatira" | | Rev 2:23 | churches | "all the churches shall know" | | Rev 2:29 | churches | "what the Spirit saith unto the churches" | | Rev 3:1 | church | "angel of the church in Sardis" | | Rev 3:6 | churches | "what the Spirit saith unto the churches" | | Rev 3:7 | church | "angel of the church in Philadelphia" | | Rev 3:13 | churches | "what the Spirit saith unto the churches" | | Rev 3:14 | church | "angel of the church of the Laodiceans" | | Rev 3:22 | churches | "what the Spirit saith unto the churches" | | Rev 22:16 | churches | "testify unto you these things in the churches" |

    KEY FINDING: ekklesia appears 20x in Rev 1-3 and then DISAPPEARS from the text entirely until Rev 22:16 (the epilogue). The word is absent from chapters 4-21.

    G3340 — μετανοέω (metanoeo) — repent¶

    Transliteration: metanoeo | Pronunciation: met-an-o-eh | POS: verb | BLB Count: 34 Definition: to think differently or afterwards; to reconsider

    Revelation Usage: | Ref | Translation | Context | |-----|------------|---------| | Rev 2:5 (x2) | repent | "repent, and do the first works...except thou repent" | | Rev 2:16 | Repent | "Repent; or else I will come unto thee quickly" | | Rev 2:21 (x2) | repent / repented | "I gave her space to repent...and she repented not" | | Rev 3:3 | repent | "hold fast, and repent" | | Rev 3:19 | repent | "be zealous therefore, and repent" | | Rev 9:20 | repented | "repented not of the works of their hands" | | Rev 16:9 | repented | "repented not to give him glory" | | Rev 16:11 | repented | "repented not of their deeds" |

    KEY FINDING: Five repentance calls to five churches (Ephesus 2:5, Pergamos 2:16, Thyatira 2:21, Sardis 3:3, Laodicea 3:19). Smyrna and Philadelphia receive NO call to repent. Later in Revelation (9:20; 16:9,11), three times people REFUSE to repent — a tragic contrast.

    G4151 — πνεῦμα (pneuma) — spirit¶

    Transliteration: pneuma | Pronunciation: pnyoo | POS: neuter noun | BLB Count: 385 Definition: a current of air, breath; by analogy, a spirit

    "Seven Spirits" Usage: - Rev 1:4 — "from the seven Spirits which are before his throne" - Rev 3:1 — "he that hath the seven Spirits of God" - Rev 4:5 — "seven lamps of fire burning before the throne, which are the seven Spirits of God" - Rev 5:6 — "seven eyes, which are the seven Spirits of God sent forth into all the earth"

    "The Spirit saith unto the churches" Formula: - Rev 2:7, 2:11, 2:17, 2:29, 3:6, 3:13, 3:22 — seven occurrences, verbatim identical

    G4735 — στέφανος (stephanos) — crown (victor's wreath)¶

    Transliteration: stephanos | Pronunciation: stef | POS: masculine noun | BLB Count: 18 Definition: a chaplet (as a badge of royalty, a prize in the public games or a symbol of honor); a wreath

    Revelation Usage: | Ref | Translation | Context | |-----|------------|---------| | Rev 2:10 | crown | "I will give thee a crown of life" | | Rev 3:11 | crown | "hold fast...that no man take thy crown" | | Rev 4:4 | crowns | "on their heads crowns of gold" (24 elders) | | Rev 4:10 | crowns | "cast their crowns before the throne" | | Rev 6:2 | crown | "a crown was given unto him" (first seal rider) | | Rev 9:7 | crowns | "on their heads were as it were crowns" (locusts) | | Rev 12:1 | crown | "upon her head a crown of twelve stars" (woman) | | Rev 14:14 | crown | "on his head a golden crown" (one like Son of man) |

    Other NT Usage: - Mat 27:29; Mrk 15:17; Jhn 19:2,5 — crown of thorns - 1Co 9:25 — "incorruptible crown" - Php 4:1; 1Th 2:19 — "crown of rejoicing" - 2Ti 4:8 — "crown of righteousness" - Jas 1:12 — "crown of life" (cf. Rev 2:10) - 1Pe 5:4 — "crown of glory"

    KEY FINDING: stephanos (victor's crown) vs. diadema (royal crown). The churches receive stephanos — the crown earned through struggle. Same crown given to first seal rider (6:2). NOT diadema, which belongs to Christ as King (Rev 19:12).

    G2064 — ἔρχομαι (erchomai) — come¶

    Transliteration: erchomai | POS: verb | BLB Count: 643 Definition: middle voice of a primary verb; to come or go

    Revelation 2-3 Usage: - Rev 2:5 — ἔρχομαί σοι (present M/P indicative) — "I am coming to you" (disciplinary) - Rev 2:16 — (same form implied) — "I will come unto thee quickly" (disciplinary) - Rev 2:25 — ἄχρι οὗ ἂν ἥξω (heko, not erchomai) — "till I come" - Rev 3:3 — ἥξω ὡς κλέπτης (heko) — "I will come as a thief" - Rev 3:11 — ἔρχομαι ταχύ (present M/P indicative) — "I am coming quickly" - Rev 3:20 — εἰσελεύσομαι (eiserchomai, future) — "I will come in to him"

    NOTE: Rev 2:25 and 3:3 actually use heko (G2240), not erchomai. Rev 3:11 uses erchomai. The shift from disciplinary erchomai (2:5, 2:16) to eschatological erchomai/heko (3:3, 3:11) marks the transition from early to later churches.
